Exploring "expected goals" models for NHL shot data

Joey Rucska

Code available on my Github.


Identifying influential shot conditions

This analysis attempts to identify which conditions have a substantial impact on shot outcomes in the National Hockey League (NHL). Presumably, there are many conditions/factors that influence outcomes, such as shot location, shot type (e.g. slapshot, wrist shot, etc.), whether the shot came off a rebound, etc. On this page, I develop a quantitative and graphical exploration of a data set representing hundreds of thousands of NHL shots, and the influence of various "conditions" on the outcomes of those shots.

Identifying the strongest factors can help inform the development of so-called expected goals (xG) models, which I explore in the study below.

Ratio-based xG model

In this analysis, I develop a simple expected goals (xG) model based on one ratio: number of goals over number of shot attempts. By determining this ratio a function of a specific set of conditions (see above), this value becomes—in theory—representative of the likelihood a given shot with those conditions will result in a goal. From here, one could use that ratio to predict the outcomes of new shots under similar conditions. On this page, develop and evaluate the performance of this model.
